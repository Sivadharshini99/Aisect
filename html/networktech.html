Understanding Network Technologies

Types of Networks

Local Area Network (LAN)

A Local Area Network (LAN) is a network that connects computers and devices in a limited geographical area, such as a home, school, or office. It is known for its high speed and low cost.

Wide Area Network (WAN)

A Wide Area Network (WAN) spans a large geographical area, often connecting multiple smaller networks like LANs. The internet is the largest example of a WAN.

Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

A Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) covers a city or a large campus. It is larger than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, and often connects several LANs in a region.

Networks like LAN, WAN, and MAN are fundamental in connecting devices and enabling communication across different geographical areas.
